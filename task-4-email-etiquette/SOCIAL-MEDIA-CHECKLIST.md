Social Media Checklist for College Students
Being a student in the digital age means our online presence is often our first impression. Here is a quick guide on how to navigate social 
media responsibly while building a positive digital footprint.


The Do’s


Audit Your Privacy Regularly: Take 5 minutes every month to check your account settings. Ensure you are only sharing personal updates with people you actually know and trust.


Think Before You Post: Ask yourself—"Would I be okay with a future employer or my professor seeing this?" If the answer is no, keep it in your drafts.


Fact-Check Every Story: Before hitting 'Share' on a viral news piece or a controversial post, verify it from a reliable source. Don't be a medium for spreading misinformation.


Network with Purpose: Use platforms like LinkedIn to follow industry leaders and VIT seniors. Engaging with their content can open doors for internships and guidance.


Secure Your Digital Identity: Use strong, unique passwords for every platform and always enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA). It’s the simplest way to prevent getting hacked.



The Dont's


Don't Overshare Sensitive Data: Never post your registration number, phone number, home address, or any financial details (like UPI IDs or QR codes) publicly.


Avoid Online Heated Debates: It’s easy to get angry in a comment section. Avoid disrespectful arguments or cyberbullying; digital footprints are permanent and can haunt your career.


Stop Clicking Suspicious Links: If a "friend" sends a weird link or you see a "too good to be true" offer, don't click it. Phishing is real and it’s fast.


Don't Violate University Guidelines: Avoid posting content that brings disrepute to your institution or peers. Respect the digital boundaries of your academic community.

Don't Let the Scroll Take Over: Social media is a tool, not a lifestyle. Don't let endless scrolling affect your sleep cycle or your focus on lectures and assignments.
